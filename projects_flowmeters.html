Overview

In collaboration with Tamas, I'm supervising and assisting in the development of an in-house designed Venturi Flowmeter project at SFU Rocketry. This project represents a significant step forward in our pursuit of cost-effective and precise fluid measurement solutions, specifically tailored for rocketry applications. The motivation behind creating our Venturi Flowmeters is twofold: to achieve a high level of self-reliance in our propulsion system feedback and control, and to push the envelope of technical advancement within our team. These flowmeters are engineered to provide accurate and reliable measurements of fluid flow, a critical factor in optimizing the performance of our engines. One of the project's most remarkable achievements is the cost efficiency of our design. Our Venturi Flowmeters are set to cost less than 10% of the price of equivalent commercial options, without compromising on precision or reliability. This breakthrough is a testament to the innovative use of physics and engineering principles in tackling real-world problems. The compact and efficient design of these flowmeters is not just about cost-saving; it's about enabling our team to build larger and more powerful engines with the capability of reaching the upper atmosphere. The project is nearing a stage where we can showcase our design, highlighting the practical application of our work and our commitment to advancing rocketry technology.
