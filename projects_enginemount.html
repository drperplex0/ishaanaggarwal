Overview

As the lead on the next-generation engine mount project at SFU Rocketry, our team's goal is to create a mount that can adapt to multiple engines of varying sizes. This endeavor is currently underway, aiming to revolutionize how we integrate engines with our test stands and the Liquid Rocket Engine Gen-II specifically. The development of this updated Engine Mount is driven by our objective to future-proof our testing platforms. It's designed to be a universal solution for all future small engines developed and tested by SFU Rocketry. Key features of the Engine Mount include a robust universal design, adjustable supports, and a large factor of safety. These attributes are critical in ensuring the mount's durability, precision, and longevity. Our work focuses on engineering a mount that not only meets the current needs of our propulsion systems but also anticipates the requirements of future projects. The adjustable nature of the supports allows for a high degree of flexibility, making it possible to test a wide range of engine sizes without the need for multiple, specialized mounts.
